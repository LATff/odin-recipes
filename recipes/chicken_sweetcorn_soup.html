Chicken and Sweetcorn soup

A warming hearty soup.

This recipe makes about 6 portions.

Chicken and sweetcorn soup. The classic, the takeaway standard, and, I'll say it, the yardstick by which the quality of a takeaway place can be measured.

However none of that matters right now, because from this moment you have the power to create this soup in your very own home. Use it wisely.

Ingredients

  • 15ml vegetable oil
  • 2 garlic cloves, peeled and finely chopped
  • 2.5cm ginger, peeled and finely chopped
  • 15ml light soy sauce
  • 2 cobs (300g) sweetcorn kernels, tinned is preferred
  • 1.5l chicken stock
  • 45ml cornflour
  • 2 cooked chicken breasts, shredded or cut into strips
  • 2 eggs, whisked
  • 15ml sesame oil
  • 2 spring onions, washed and finely sliced

    • Preparation

    1. Fry the garlic and ginger in the oil for 1 minute in a large saucepan.
    2. Add the sweetcorn and soy sauce then fry together for 3 minutes.
    3. Pour in the stock and let the soup bubble.
    4. Create a slurry with the cornflour by adding a bit of water with the flour in a cup.
    5. Slowly add the slurry in a thin drizzle to the soup while stirring. The soup should thicken after a short while
    6. Stir in the chicken.
    7. Just like you did with the slurry, add the whisked eggs to the soup while stirring.
    8. Finish off with a splash of sesame oil and after dividing the soup between bowls, sprinkle with spring onion. Serve immediately.
